What is a bitcoin faucet?
A bitcoin faucet is a web page that gives small amounts of bitcoins or satoshis for simply entering your email wallet address and solving a captcha.

What is bitcoin?
Bitcoin is a form virtual currency, it's been called digital currency. It was designed and created to be held electronically over the internet. It's the internet currency. No one controls it. Bitcoins aren’t printed like conventional currency, like dollars or euros - they’re produced by people, and increasingly businesses, running computers across the world, using software that solves mathematical problems.
It was created by a software programmer known as Satoshi Nakamoto. He proposed it as an electronic payment system based on mathematical proof. The idea was to produce a currency independent of any central authority, transferable electronically, more or less instantly, with very low transaction fees.

What is Satoshi?
A satoshi is currently the smallest unit of the Bitcoin currency supported by the block chain (1/100,000,000 BTC or 0.00000001 BTC). The unit has been named "satoshi" in collective homage to the founder Bitcoin, Satoshi Nakamoto.
